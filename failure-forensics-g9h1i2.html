  • THE FLAW · THE LUCK FALLACY

    Most guys blame “bad timing” or “she wasn’t feeling it.” That victim narrative kills progress.

    “Every conversation leaves evidence. Engineers diagnose; gamblers blame chance. Pick your tribe.”

  • DOCTRINE · STRUCTURE OVER CHANCE

    Great conversations aren’t magic. They stand on solid architecture. When one crashes, a core beam failed.

    The 3-Tier Architecture

    • Tier 1: Presence – first-impression body language, voice, eye contact.
    • Tier 2: Loop – the conversational engine; balanced exchange.
    • Tier 3: Direction – a clear next step that keeps momentum.

  • PROTOCOL · 3-CRASH AUTOPSY

    Run a forensic analysis on your last three conversation crashes. Stay objective—emotion clouds data.

    Autopsy #1

    Summarise the crash in one sentence. Which tier collapsed—Presence, Loop, or Direction?

    Autopsy #2

    Repeat the process for your second crash.

    Autopsy #3

    Do it once more. Record the primary failure point.

  • PROTOCOL · PATTERN RECOGNITION

    Lay the three autopsies side-by-side. Hunt for the biggest leak.

    “Fix one critical weakness and you’ll outperform any stack of new tricks.”

  • THE DRILL · 7-DAY ANALYSIS SPRINT

    For one week, log a key interaction each day—good or bad—and tag the strongest and weakest tiers.

    Day 1
    Log one interaction. Tag the strongest tier.
    Day 2
    Log one interaction. Tag the weakest tier.
    Day 3
    Repeat Day 1.
    Day 4
    Repeat Day 2.
    Day 5
    Review logs. Identify the recurring weak tier.
    Day 6
    Run one interaction—focus only on that weak tier.
    Day 7
    Repeat Day 6. Measure improvement.
