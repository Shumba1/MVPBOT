  • MODULE 1

    The Laws of Frame

    Understanding the Invisible Battlefield

  • THE PRIME DIRECTIVE

    Frame is your subjective reality. It’s your worldview—your assumptions and unspoken rules. When two people interact, two frames collide.

    The man who gets nervous, seeks approval, and mirrors someone else’s mood holds a weak frame. A master acts upon the world—never the reverse.

  • FRAME VS. POSTURING

    Don’t confuse loud posturing with true frame.

    Posturing (Weak)

    • Brittle & defensive
    • Arrogance rooted in insecurity
    • Needs constant validation
    • Over-reacts to challenges

    Frame (Strong)

    • Flexible, rooted in self-belief
    • Quiet authority
    • Self-validation
    • Treats tests as entertainment

    Posturing is cardboard armor. Frame is a gravitational field.

  • MODULE 2

    The Four Pillars

    Forging Your Foundation

  • PILLAR I · SELF-POSSESSION

    Outcome Independence

    Your emotional state is not for sale. It does not hinge on anyone’s approval.

    “I feel great. If she’s receptive, she can share in it. If not, I still feel great.”

  • PILLAR II · ASSUMED VALUE

    Quiet Authority

    Operate from the premise that you’re the selector. That’s confidence, not arrogance.

    Shift: Stop wondering “Will she like me?” Start wondering “Will I like her?”

  • PILLAR III · INTENTIONALITY

    Purpose Over People

    A mission bigger than any single woman eliminates neediness and creates magnetic pull.

    Your life’s purpose is the bedrock of your frame. She’s a welcome guest, not the center.

  • PILLAR IV · AMUSED DETACHMENT

    Indifference to Drama

    Treat pressure and tests as playful signals, not threats. The un-rattleable man is the trustworthy man.

    You’re the calm observer of the human comedy.

  • MODULE 3

    Frame in Action

    Tactical Application

  • HANDLING THE TEST

    Test: “I bet you say that to all the girls.”

    • Weak Frame: “No, you’re different!” (defensive)
    • Strong Frame: Only the interesting ones. Jury’s still out on you. (smirk, amused)

  • FRAME RE-CAPTURE

    You will slip. Masters reset instantly.

    3-Step Reset

    1. Notice: “Frame slipped.” (no self-blame)
    2. Breathe: One slow diaphragm breath.
    3. Re-assert: Next action from solid frame—question, humor, or redirect.
